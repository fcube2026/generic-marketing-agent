## 1. Executive Summary

Curex24 is an on-demand healthcare marketplace connecting patients with nearby verified healthcare providers for home visits and clinic consultations. The marketing launch strategy focuses on a **geography-first, community-driven approach** — establishing trust and adoption in 2–3 pilot locations before scaling.

**Launch Philosophy:**
- **Rural-first:** Start in underserved areas where the pain point is most acute
- **Provider-led:** A strong provider network drives patient trust
- **Community-embedded:** Partner with local health infrastructure (PHCs, ASHA workers, pharmacies)
- **Digital + ground:** Combine online marketing with on-the-ground activation

## 2. Target Market & Personas

### 2.1 Primary Geography

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Phase 1 (Launch)** | 2–3 district headquarters / semi-urban towns in one state |
| **Phase 2 (3 months)** | Expand to 5–8 towns in 2 states |
| **Phase 3 (6 months)** | Multi-state presence, 15–20 towns |
| **Ideal launch towns** | Population 50K–300K, limited specialist access, smartphone penetration >60% |

### 2.2 Patient Personas

#### Persona 1: "Rural Rajesh" — The Concerned Parent
- **Age:** 30–45 | **Location:** Semi-urban / rural
- **Need:** Quick medical consultation for family, especially children and elderly parents
- **Pain point:** Nearest specialist is 30+ km away; can't take time off work for hospital visits
- **Behaviour:** Uses WhatsApp, watches YouTube; trusts word-of-mouth from community
- **Entry point:** Urgent need (child's fever, elderly parent's chronic condition)

#### Persona 2: "Mobility-Limited Maya" — The Elderly Patient
- **Age:** 60+ | **Location:** Town / peri-urban
- **Need:** Regular health checkups, physiotherapy, chronic disease management
- **Pain point:** Difficulty traveling to clinics; dependent on family for transport
- **Behaviour:** Phone used with family help; decision-maker is adult child
- **Entry point:** Home visit for physiotherapy or follow-up consultation

#### Persona 3: "Busy Priya" — The Working Professional
- **Age:** 25–40 | **Location:** Semi-urban / small city
- **Need:** Convenient consultation without waiting in clinic queues
- **Pain point:** Limited time; wants quick, reliable healthcare
- **Behaviour:** Active on Instagram, Google Maps; compares prices online
- **Entry point:** First-time booking for a general checkup or minor ailment

### 2.3 Provider Personas

#### Persona A: "Dr. Arun" — The Part-Time Practitioner
- **Age:** 30–50 | **Type:** General physician
- **Need:** Earn extra income during off-clinic hours
- **Pain point:** Underutilized capacity; limited patient reach
- **Motivation:** Flexible schedule, guaranteed payments, patient flow

#### Persona B: "Sunita the Specialist"
- **Age:** 28–45 | **Type:** Physiotherapist / speech therapist
- **Need:** Build a client base for home-visit services
- **Pain point:** No platform to discover and connect with patients needing home services
- **Motivation:** Direct access to patients, professional visibility, digital record keeping

## 3. Positioning & Messaging

### 3.1 Brand Positioning

> **Curex24 — Your doctor, at your door. 24 hours ready.**

**Core Position:** The most accessible way to get trusted, verified healthcare — whether a doctor comes to you or you visit them — powered by smart matching and real-time availability.

### 3.2 Key Messages

| Audience | Message | Emotion |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Patients (general) | "Skip the queue. A verified doctor is just a tap away." | Convenience |
| Patients (rural) | "No more traveling for hours. Healthcare comes home." | Relief |
| Patients (elderly) | "Doctor at your doorstep. Care without compromise." | Trust |
| Providers | "Fill your empty hours. Earn more. Help more." | Opportunity |
| Community leaders | "Bring modern healthcare to your community." | Pride |

### 3.3 Brand Voice

- **Warm** — approachable, caring, not corporate
- **Simple** — avoid medical jargon; speak the patient's language
- **Trustworthy** — emphasize verified providers, transparent pricing
- **Local** — regional language support, local testimonials

## 4. Pre-Launch Phase (T-8 to T-4 Weeks)

### 4.1 Provider Network Building (T-8 to T-4)

This is the most critical pre-launch activity. Patients will not adopt without providers.

| Activity | Timeline | Owner | Details |
|----------|----------|-------|---------|
| Identify pilot geography | T-8 | Product | Select 2–3 towns based on demographics, provider density, smartphone penetration |
| Map local providers | T-8 to T-6 | Growth | Create database of GPs, specialists, therapists, nurses in pilot areas |
| Provider outreach (phone/visit) | T-7 to T-5 | Growth | Personal outreach to top 30–50 providers per town |
| Provider onboarding sessions | T-5 to T-4 | Ops | Conduct group/individual sessions: app walkthrough, KYC submission, fee configuration |
| Provider activation | T-4 | Ops | Ensure ≥10 active providers per town before patient marketing begins |

### 4.2 Digital Presence Setup (T-6 to T-4)

| Create app store listings (screenshots, descriptions, keywords) | T-5 | Marketing |

## 8. Provider Acquisition Strategy

### 8.1 Provider Value Proposition

> "Earn ₹2,000–₹5,000+ extra per week. See patients during your free hours. We handle payments, scheduling, and records."

### 8.2 Acquisition Channels

| Channel | Approach | Expected Yield |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Direct outreach (phone/visit) | Personal visits to clinics in pilot area | 30–40% conversion |
| IMA chapter meetings | Present at local medical association meetings | 10–15 providers per session |
| WhatsApp doctor groups | Share opportunity in regional doctor networks | 5–10% conversion |
| Medical conferences | Booth/presentation at state-level conferences | Brand awareness + leads |
| Provider referral | "Refer a colleague, earn ₹500" bonus | 20–30% of new providers |
| LinkedIn | Targeted outreach to therapists, specialists | Low conversion but quality leads |
| Medical colleges | Campus outreach for young doctors | Future pipeline |

### 8.3 Provider Onboarding Flow

1. **Contact:** Phone call or in-person visit explaining the opportunity
2. **Demo:** Walkthrough of the app (booking acceptance, consultation flow, earnings)
3. **Sign-up:** App install, profile creation, KYC document upload
4. **Verification:** Admin verifies credentials within 24–48 hours
5. **Activation:** Provider sets availability, fee structure, service modes
6. **First booking:** Guided support for first 3 bookings

### 8.4 Provider Retention

| Strategy | Details |
|----------|---------|
| Fast payouts | 80% fee credited within 48 hours |
| Performance bonuses | Bonus for completing 10/25/50 bookings |
| Priority listing | Top-rated providers appear first in recommendations |
| Professional development | Free CME (Continuing Medical Education) content |
| Community | Quarterly provider meetups, WhatsApp support group |
| Dashboard | Earnings analytics, patient satisfaction scores |

## 9. Patient Acquisition Strategy

### 9.1 Acquisition Funnel

```
AWARENESS → INTEREST → INSTALL → REGISTER → FIRST BOOKING → REPEAT
   100%       40%       20%       15%         8%              4%
```

### 9.2 Acquisition Channels by Stage

| Stage | Channel | Tactic |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Awareness | Social media, posters, health camps, word-of-mouth | Brand exposure |
| Interest | WhatsApp content, YouTube videos, pharmacy referral | Education |
| Install | App store ads, QR codes (posters, pharmacy), referral link | Direct CTA |
| Register | In-app onboarding (phone + OTP — frictionless) | Simplified UX |
| First Booking | Launch offer (first booking free), push notification | Incentive |
| Repeat | Health tips push, booking reminders, referral rewards | Engagement |

### 9.3 Referral Program

| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Mechanic | Share referral code → friend books → both get ₹100 credit |
| Limit | Max 10 referrals per user (₹1,000 cap) |
| Tracking | Unique referral code per user, tracked in-app |
| Promotion | Prominent in-app card, WhatsApp share button |
| Anti-fraud | Same device detection, minimum booking value for credit |
