# Best-in-Class AI Marketing Agent — Complete Design

A complete, execution-ready design for an AI Marketing Agent that can own end-to-end marketing — strategy, planning, execution, optimization, and reporting — for any business.

---

## 1. Marketing Agent Purpose

**Name (working):** CMO-Agent (a.k.a. "The Operator").

**Mission:** Act as an always-on Chief Marketing Officer + execution operator that:

1. Deeply understands the business, market, and customer.
2. Builds and maintains a living marketing strategy.
3. Translates that strategy into concrete weekly/daily execution.
4. Produces ready-to-ship marketing assets (briefs, copy, calendars, sequences).
5. Runs a continuous test → learn → optimize loop using analytics.
6. Coordinates humans, tools, and sub-agents under clear approval checkpoints.

**Operating principle:** *Strategy is worthless without shipped work; execution is wasteful without strategy.* The agent must always link every task back to a goal, KPI, and hypothesis.

**Design tenets:**
- Bias to action with measurable outcomes.
- MVP-first marketing → scale what works → kill what doesn't.
- Brand-safe, on-message, compliant by default.
- Transparent reasoning, auditable decisions, human-in-the-loop on high-risk actions.
- Compounding assets (SEO, content, lifecycle, community) prioritized over rented attention alone.

## 3. Inputs Required from the Business

The agent maintains a **Business Context Pack (BCP)** — a structured, evolving document.

**3.1 Company basics** — legal/brand name, founding story, mission, vision, values; stage; funding/runway; monthly marketing budget cap.

**3.2 Offering** — product/service description; SKUs, plans, pricing, margins; jobs-to-be-done; onboarding/activation flow.

**3.3 Market** — category and sub-category; geography; TAM estimate; market maturity; regulatory constraints; top 5–10 competitors + indirect alternatives.

**3.4 Customer** — ICPs (firmographic for B2B / demographic + psychographic for B2C); personas with pains, gains, triggers, objections, buying process; CRM exports, NPS, churn reasons, top accounts; voice-of-customer artifacts.

**3.5 Brand** — current logo, colors, typography, design system; tone of voice, do/don't lists, banned terms; existing brand perception.

**3.6 Current marketing state** — channels in use + spend + 6–12 months of performance; tech stack (CRM, ESP, CDP, analytics, ad platforms, CMS, social tools); team and agencies; current funnel data (visits, leads, MQLs, SQLs, customers, ARPU, LTV, CAC, payback, churn).

**3.7 Goals and constraints** — business goals (revenue, users, GMV, retention) for 12/24 months; marketing OKRs; budget by quarter; hiring plan; compliance (GDPR, HIPAA, financial promotions, regional ad rules); no-go list.

**3.8 Access & integrations** — read access to: GA4/analytics, ad platforms, CRM, ESP, CMS, social accounts, search console, BI warehouse; write-access scope (draft only vs. publish), per channel.

## 4. Questions the Agent Should Ask First

The agent runs a structured **Discovery Interview** in 6 short sessions.

**Session 1 — Business & Model**
1. In one sentence, what does your business do and for whom?
2. What is your business model and primary revenue driver today?
3. What stage are you in (pre-PMF, early PMF, scaling, mature)?
4. What does success look like in 12 months? In 3 months?
5. What is your monthly marketing budget today, and what's the ceiling?

**Session 2 — Customer**
6. Who are your top 1–3 ICPs? What do your best customers have in common?
7. What triggers them to look for a solution like yours?
8. What are the top 3 objections you hear?
9. Where do they spend time online and offline?
10. Can you share 5–10 customer interview transcripts, reviews, or calls?

**Session 3 — Offering & Differentiation**
11. What problem do you solve, and what's the cost of *not* solving it?
12. What are the top 3 alternatives customers consider (including "do nothing")?
13. Why do customers choose you? Why do they leave?
14. What can you do that competitors structurally cannot?

**Session 4 — Brand & Message**
15. What is your current brand promise, in your own words?
16. What 3 words should describe your brand? What 3 must it never be?
17. Do you have brand/visual guidelines? Share them.

**Session 5 — Current Marketing**
18. What channels are working / not working today, and how do you know?
19. Share the last 6–12 months of channel-level performance.
20. What experiments have you tried that failed? Why do you think they failed?
21. What's your current funnel conversion rate at each stage?
22. What's your CAC, payback period, LTV, gross margin?

**Session 6 — Constraints & Operating Model**
23. Who's on the marketing team and what can each person ship per week?
24. What approvals do legal/founders require before publishing?
25. What tools are in your stack? Where are integration gaps?
26. What is off-limits (claims, channels, regions, competitors)?
27. What is the single biggest marketing problem I should solve in 90 days?

The agent also performs **autonomous discovery in parallel**: scrape website, analyze competitors, mine reviews (G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, App Store, Reddit), pull search trends, audit social presence, run a technical SEO crawl, and audit existing analytics setup.

## 5. Full Marketing Strategy Framework

Each module has a 1-page summary + a deep-dive appendix.

### 5.1 Situation Analysis
- Market & category snapshot, trends, regulatory factors.
- Competitive matrix (positioning, pricing, messaging, channels, share of voice).
- SWOT + "5 Forces lite".
- Customer insight synthesis (jobs, pains, gains, triggers, objections).
- Current marketing audit with health scores.

### 5.2 Brand & Positioning
- Positioning statement (audience, category, unique value, evidence).
- Brand narrative & origin story.
- Messaging house: core promise → 3 messaging pillars → proof points → audience-specific variants.
- Tone of voice rubric with examples (do/don't).
- Naming & visual guidance (when relevant).

### 5.3 Audience & Segmentation
- Segment map by value, intent, lifecycle stage.
- ICP scorecards (firmographic, behavioral, intent signals).
- Persona one-pagers: pains, JTBD, content preferences, channels, objections, buying committee (B2B).

### 5.4 GTM Strategy
- Motion (PLG, sales-led, community-led, hybrid) per segment.
- Acquisition → activation → monetization → retention → referral path per segment.
- Pricing/packaging implications.

### 5.5 Channel Strategy
- Channel scoring matrix (fit, cost, speed-to-impact, scalability, defensibility).
- Primary, secondary, experimental channel tiers.
- Budget allocation logic with re-allocation rules (e.g., 70/20/10 known/promising/experimental).

### 5.6 Content Strategy
- Content pillars (3–5) tied to positioning.
- Format mix per stage: TOFU/MOFU/BOFU.
- Editorial calendar cadence by channel.
- Repurposing graph (1 pillar asset → N derivative assets).
- Founder/SME content engine where applicable.

### 5.7 SEO Strategy
- Topical authority map; pillar-cluster architecture.
- Keyword universe (head, mid, long-tail, branded, comparison, JTBD).
- Technical SEO baseline & fix list.
- Internal linking, schema, content refresh plan.
- Off-page: digital PR, link-worthy assets, partnerships.

### 5.8 Paid Acquisition Strategy
- Channel-by-channel plan (Google Search, PMax, Meta, LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, retargeting, programmatic).
- Campaign architecture (account → campaign → ad set → ad).
- Audience strategy (cold, warm, hot, lookalikes, retargeting tiers).
- Creative testing framework (concepts × hooks × formats).
- Bidding, budget pacing, frequency caps, exclusions.
- Landing page and post-click strategy.

### 5.9 Social Media Strategy
- Platform-specific roles (e.g., LinkedIn = authority, TikTok = reach, IG = brand, X = real-time).
- Content formats and cadence.
- Engagement playbook (replies, DMs, comments).
- Community moderation guidelines.

### 5.10 Influencer / Creator Strategy
- Tiering (nano, micro, macro, mega) and use cases.
- Brief templates, deliverables, usage rights, compensation models.
- Discovery and vetting rubric; outreach sequences.
- Always-on vs. campaign bursts.

### 5.11 Email / CRM / Lifecycle Strategy
- Lifecycle map: Subscribe → Welcome → Activate → Engage → Convert → Retain → Expand → Win-back.
- Triggered flows + broadcasts cadence.
- Segmentation strategy, suppression rules, deliverability hygiene.
- Personalization and dynamic content rules.

### 5.12 Partnership Strategy
- Partner archetypes (integration, channel, co-marketing, reseller, association).
- Partner ICP scorecard, shortlist, and outreach plan.
- Co-marketing playbooks (webinars, joint content, bundles).

### 5.13 Referral Strategy
- Eligibility, incentive design (double-sided, tiered, milestone).
- Trigger points in product and lifecycle.
- Anti-fraud, attribution, and reporting.

### 5.14 Retention Strategy
- Activation definition and "aha moment" engineering.
- Engagement loops, habit triggers, education programs.
- Churn diagnosis: voluntary vs. involuntary; pre-empt vs. recover.
- Customer marketing: case studies, advocacy, community.

### 5.15 PLG Opportunities (if applicable)
- Free/freemium/trial design; activation metric.
- In-product growth surfaces (empty states, invites, share, upsell).
- PQL definition and handoff.

### 5.16 Launch Strategy
- Tiered launches (silent, beta, soft, full, anniversary).
- Pre-launch (waitlist, teaser), launch day (press, social, email, paid), post-launch (drumbeat).
- Asset checklist; war room cadence.

### 5.17 Demand Generation Engine
- Always-on demand creation (organic + paid + community + PR).
- Demand capture (SEO BOFU, branded paid, comparison pages, review sites).
- MQL/PQL pipeline targets and conversion math.

## 6. Execution Framework

### 6.1 Goal Cascade
- **North Star Metric** (e.g., weekly active paying customers).
- **Annual Marketing OKRs** (3–5).
- **Quarterly OKRs** with key results tied to acquisition/activation/retention/revenue.
- **Monthly themes** (e.g., "Activation lift month").
- **Weekly priorities** (max 3 per pod).

### 6.2 Planning Cadence
- **Quarterly Plan**: budget, OKRs, big bets, campaign roadmap, hiring/agency needs.
- **Monthly Plan**: campaigns, content calendar, experiments, channel targets, budget pacing.
- **Weekly Plan**: shipping list, owners, dependencies, risks, review of last week's results.
- **Daily Standup (async)**: agent posts what's shipping, blockers, decisions needed.

### 6.3 Backlog & Prioritization
- Single source of truth backlog with: hypothesis, expected impact, effort, confidence, owner, dependencies, channel, KPI affected, status.
- Scoring: **RICE** for projects, **ICE** for experiments.
- WIP limits per channel/owner to prevent thrash.

### 6.4 Campaign Lifecycle (standard loop)
1. **Brief** (problem, audience, message, offer, KPI, budget, deadline).
2. **Plan** (channels, assets, owners, calendar).
3. **Build** (assets created/approved).
4. **Launch** (QA checklist, tracking verified).
5. **Monitor** (daily for first 7 days).
6. **Optimize** (creative refresh, bid/budget shift, audience tune).
7. **Review** (post-mortem with what worked, why, and what to scale/kill).
8. **Productize** (turn winners into SOPs/templates).

### 6.5 Experimentation Loop
- Hypothesis: "If we do X for audience Y, metric Z will move by N% because…"
- Design: control vs. variant, sample size, duration, success threshold.
- Run, monitor guardrails, analyze, document in **Experiment Library**.
- Decide: scale, iterate, kill.

### 6.6 Channel Action Plans
For each active channel, the agent maintains:
- Objective and KPI targets.
- Campaign architecture and audiences.
- Weekly creative refresh cadence.
- Budget pacing rules and reallocation triggers (e.g., "if CAC > 1.3× target for 7 days, reduce spend 30%").
- Owner and approval workflow.

### 6.7 Stage-Adaptive Strategy
- **Pre-PMF**: founder-led content, manual outreach, community presence, qualitative learning, no paid scale.
- **Early PMF**: double down on the 1–2 channels showing CAC < target, build SEO/content compounders, start lifecycle basics.
- **Scaling**: diversify channels, formal experimentation, lifecycle depth, partnerships, brand investment.
- **Mature**: brand + performance balance, MMM, category leadership, international expansion, retention/expansion focus.

| Daily | Inbox triage for community/DMs | Suggested replies for human approval |
| Weekly | Content calendar review + next week's shipping list | Updated calendar, briefs assigned |
| Weekly | Paid optimization review: pacing, CAC, creative fatigue | Bid/budget changes (proposed), creative refresh tasks |
| Weekly | SEO opportunity scan: rankings, CTR, new keywords, content decay | Refresh list, new content briefs |
| Weekly | Lifecycle performance review: open/CTR/conversion, deliverability | Variant tests, segmentation tweaks |
| Weekly | Experiment review: results, decisions, next experiments | Updated experiment library |
| Weekly | Marketing standup notes | Decisions log, next-week plan |
| Bi-weekly | Competitor teardown of one competitor | Insight memo |
| Monthly | Full performance report vs. OKRs | Exec dashboard + narrative |
| Monthly | Backlog grooming and re-prioritization | Updated backlog |
| Monthly | Content audit (top/bottom performers) | Refresh / consolidate / retire list |
| Quarterly | Strategy refresh, budget reallocation | Updated strategy doc + plan |
| Quarterly | Brand health check (NPS, sentiment, share of voice) | Brand scorecard |

### 7.2 Event-Driven Automations
- **New customer**: trigger onboarding flow, request review at day 14, NPS at day 60.
- **Trial signup**: kick off activation sequence personalized by use case.
- **Feature launch announcement**: auto-generate cross-channel asset pack from a single brief.
- **Support theme spike**: auto-create content brief addressing the issue.
- **Negative review**: alert + draft response for human approval.
- **Competitor pricing change**: comparison page + paid copy refresh task.
- **Trending topic match**: draft reactive post with brand-safe angle.
- **CAC drift > threshold**: alert + recommended channel reallocation.
- **Lead form submission (high-intent)**: route to sales + tailored nurture.

### 7.3 Asset Production Pipelines
- Pillar content → derivative pipeline: 1 long-form → 5 social posts → 1 newsletter → 1 video script → 3 ad variants → SEO snippets.
- Customer interview → case study + testimonial card + sales one-pager + landing page proof block.
- Webinar → recording → highlight clips → blog recap → email replay → SDR follow-up sequence.

### 7.4 Approval Gates
- Auto-publish: minor social replies, A/B copy variants within an approved framework, paid bid/budget shifts within ±20% guardrails, experiment kills.
- Human-required: brand-level messaging changes, new channel launches, budget changes >20%, public statements, partnerships, PR pitches, anything legal-sensitive.

## 9. KPIs and Reporting Structure

### 9.1 KPI Tree
- **North Star**: business-specific (e.g., Weekly Paying Users, MRR, GMV, Active Patients).
- **L1 (Marketing-owned)**: Pipeline created, SQLs, signups, paid trials, revenue influenced, CAC, payback, LTV/CAC, retention rate, NPS, share of voice.
- **L2 (Funnel)**: Visits → leads → MQL → SQL → won; activation rate; D1/D7/D30 retention; referral rate.
- **L3 (Channel)**: CPM, CPC, CTR, CVR, CPA, CAC by channel; SEO sessions, rankings, CTR; email open/click/conversion/deliverability; social reach, engagement rate, follower growth, save/share rates; influencer ROAS; PR coverage and SoV.
- **L4 (Asset)**: page-level conversion, asset engagement, scroll depth, bounce.

Every campaign/asset declares which L3/L4 metric it moves and how it ladders to L1/North Star.

### 9.2 Attribution
- Default: blended view = platform-reported + GA4/warehouse + self-reported attribution ("How did you hear about us?").
- B2B: multi-touch (W-shaped) for pipeline; first-touch for awareness; last-touch for capture.
- Scaled: MMM-lite (incrementality tests, geo holdouts) once spend > meaningful threshold.
- Always pair with **incrementality tests** before scaling decisions.

### 9.3 Reporting Cadence
- **Daily pulse**: anomalies, spend pacing, top movers.
- **Weekly scorecard**: channel performance vs. target, experiments status, next week's bets.
- **Monthly business review**: OKR progress, wins/losses, pipeline, CAC/LTV, narrative + decisions needed.
- **Quarterly review**: strategy validation, budget reallocation, roadmap update.

### 9.4 Dashboards
- Executive dashboard: North Star, revenue, CAC, payback, LTV, pipeline.
- Channel dashboards: paid, SEO, social, email, referral, partnerships.
- Funnel dashboard: stage-by-stage conversion, drop-offs, cohort retention.
- Experiment dashboard: active tests, results, decisions log.
- Brand dashboard: SoV, sentiment, branded search, NPS.

### 9.5 Decision Rules (examples the agent enforces)
- Scale a channel only after 2 consecutive weeks at CAC ≤ target *and* a positive incrementality signal.
- Refresh creative when CTR drops >25% from rolling 14-day baseline or frequency >3.
- Kill an experiment if guardrail metric (e.g., unsubscribe rate, CAC) breaches threshold.
- Reallocate budget monthly using marginal CAC, not average CAC.

## 10. Recommended Agent Architecture

### 10.1 Core Modules (Sub-Agents)
1. **Discovery Agent** — interviews, research, audits, builds/refreshes the Business Context Pack.
2. **Strategy Agent** — produces and updates strategy artifacts (positioning, GTM, channel mix).
3. **Planner Agent** — converts strategy into quarterly/monthly/weekly plans, manages backlog and prioritization.
4. **Content Agent** — generates briefs, long-form, blog, scripts, repurposing.
5. **SEO Agent** — keyword research, topical maps, on-page briefs, technical audits, content refresh.
6. **Paid Media Agent** — campaign architecture, audience, creative variants, bid/budget recommendations, pacing.
7. **Social Agent** — platform-native posts, engagement queues, trend monitoring.
8. **Lifecycle Agent** — flow design, copy, segmentation, deliverability checks.
9. **Community & Influencer Agent** — discovery, outreach, briefs, performance tracking.
10. **Partnerships & PR Agent** — target lists, pitches, co-marketing plans.
11. **CRO/Funnel Agent** — funnel diagnosis, page hypotheses, A/B test plans.
12. **Analytics Agent** — pulls data, computes KPIs, anomaly detection, builds reports.
13. **Experimentation Agent** — designs tests, monitors guardrails, writes up learnings.
14. **Brand & Compliance Guardrail Agent** — reviews any outbound asset for tone, claims, legal, and brand fit; can block publish.
15. **Orchestrator Agent (CMO)** — routes work, resolves conflicts, manages approvals, owns the goal cascade and weekly cadence.

### 10.2 Memory & Context
- **Long-term memory (vector + structured DB):**
  - Business Context Pack (BCP).
  - Brand & messaging house.
  - Customer/persona library.
  - Competitor library.
  - Asset library (every shipped asset with metadata and performance).
  - Experiment library.
  - SOP library.
  - Decision log (why we did/didn't do something).
- **Working memory:** active campaigns, current week's plan, open approvals, anomalies.
- **Episodic memory:** weekly/monthly retrospectives feeding back into strategy.

### 10.3 Tool / System Integrations
- **Analytics & data**: GA4, Mixpanel/Amplitude, Search Console, BI warehouse (BigQuery/Snowflake), Looker/Metabase.
- **Ads**: Google Ads, Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, YouTube, Reddit, programmatic DSPs.
- **CMS**: WordPress/Webflow/Contentful/Headless.
- **CRM & MAP**: HubSpot/Salesforce/Marketo/Customer.io/Braze/Klaviyo.
- **Email/SMS/Push**: ESP of record + transactional provider.
- **Social**: Buffer/Hootsuite/Sprout/Later; native APIs.
- **SEO**: Ahrefs/Semrush/Sistrix; Screaming Frog; Search Console.
- **Design/Creative**: Figma, Adobe, Canva; Runway/ElevenLabs/HeyGen for AI media.
- **Project/Workflow**: Linear/Asana/Notion/ClickUp; Slack/MS Teams.
- **Listening & PR**: Brand24/Mention/Meltwater/Muck Rack.
- **Data ingestion/ETL**: Fivetran/Airbyte; reverse ETL via Hightouch/Census.
- **Experimentation**: GrowthBook/Optimizely/VWO; in-product flags via LaunchDarkly.

### 10.4 Workflow Stages (per task)
Intake → Context fetch → Plan → Generate → Self-critique → Brand/Compliance check → Human approval (if required) → Publish/Schedule → Track → Learn → Update memory.

### 10.5 Approval Checkpoints
- **Tier 0 (auto)**: low-risk replies, copy variants within an approved template, in-guardrail bid/budget changes.
- **Tier 1 (lightweight human review)**: scheduled posts, emails to existing lists, blog publishes.
- **Tier 2 (formal approval)**: new campaigns, new channels, brand statements, partnerships, PR.
- **Tier 3 (executive sign-off)**: strategy changes, budget shifts >20%, crisis comms.

### 10.6 Reporting Structure
- Orchestrator publishes daily digest, weekly scorecard, monthly review.
- Each sub-agent produces a section; Orchestrator synthesizes narrative + decisions needed.
- All artifacts versioned; decisions and rationale stored in the decision log.

### 10.7 Continuous Improvement Loops
- **Campaign loop** (weekly): brief → ship → measure → optimize → document.
- **Experiment loop** (bi-weekly): hypothesize → test → learn → productize.
- **Strategy loop** (quarterly): outcomes vs. plan → adjust positioning, channels, budget.
- **Brand loop** (quarterly): perception/sentiment/SoV → narrative refresh.

### 10.8 Safety, Quality, and Governance
- Brand & compliance guardrail agent vetoes off-brand or non-compliant assets.
- Hallucination controls: every claim must be backed by a source in memory or flagged.
- PII handling: customer data isolated; no raw PII in prompts; tokenized references only.
- Audit log: every published asset and every spend decision is traceable.
- Kill switch: human can pause any agent or channel instantly.

## 11. 30-60-90 Day Rollout Plan

### Days 0–30 — Discover, Diagnose, Establish Foundations

Goals: Build the BCP, fix tracking, ship the first 2–3 quick wins.

**Week 1 — Setup & Discovery**
- Run discovery interviews; collect data; ingest existing assets.
- Connect read-access to analytics, ads, CRM, ESP, CMS, social.
- Stand up backlog, decision log, asset library, experiment library.
- Audits (in parallel): brand, website, SEO technical, paid accounts, lifecycle, analytics tracking, social.

**Week 2 — Diagnose & Prioritize**
- Deliver: situation analysis, competitor teardown, funnel diagnosis, customer insight memo.
- Identify top 5 quick wins (e.g., fix conversion tracking, refresh top landing page, launch welcome email, kill a wasteful campaign, publish 1 BOFU comparison page).
- Draft positioning and messaging house v1.

**Week 3 — Strategy v1 & Tracking Fixes**
- Deliver: 1-page strategy v1 + 90-day plan.
- Fix tracking, set up dashboards (exec, channel, funnel).
- Approve brand/compliance guardrails.
- Begin shipping quick wins.

**Week 4 — First Campaign**
- Launch first integrated campaign tied to top business goal.
- Stand up weekly cadence: standup, scorecard, experiment review.
- Establish content production pipeline (1 pillar + repurposing).

**Exit criteria for Day 30:** BCP complete and signed off; strategy v1 and 90-day plan approved; tracking + dashboards live; 2–3 quick wins shipped with measured impact; one campaign live and being optimized.

### Days 31–60 — Build the Engine

Goals: Stand up the always-on engines for SEO, content, lifecycle, and paid; expand experiments.

- **SEO engine**: publish topical map, ship 4–8 cluster articles, fix top 10 technical issues, start link/PR plan.
- **Content engine**: 4 pillar pieces + 20+ derivatives; founder content cadence if applicable.
- **Lifecycle engine**: welcome, activation, post-purchase, re-engagement, win-back flows live.
- **Paid engine**: rebuild account structure; launch 2–3 hypothesis-driven creative concepts × 3 hooks each; instrument incrementality tests.
- **Social engine**: platform-specific calendars; daily engagement queue.
- **Experimentation**: 4–6 tests in flight (LP, ad creative, email subject lines, onboarding step).
- **Reporting**: weekly scorecard cadence locked in; first monthly business review delivered.

**Exit criteria for Day 60:** all core engines operational; ≥1 channel hitting CAC target; activation or conversion lift demonstrated on a key funnel step; experiment library has documented learnings.

### Days 61–90 — Scale, Compound, and Operationalize

Goals: Scale what works, deepen retention, formalize ops, validate strategy.

- **Scale paid** on validated creatives/audiences; cut underperformers; add 1 new channel test.
- **SEO compounding**: continue cluster build-out; refresh top decayed posts; secure first wave of links.
- **Lifecycle depth**: segment-specific flows; lift activation and retention; launch referral v1.
- **Partnerships/PR**: 3–5 partner conversations in motion; first PR placements pitched; co-marketing pilot.
- **Community**: if relevant, launch or scale community surface (Slack/Discord/Circle/forum).
- **CRO**: ship 3+ winning tests on top pages; codify into design system.
- **Operations**: SOPs for top 10 workflows; agency/contractor briefs if needed.
- **Strategy review**: validate or adjust positioning, channel mix, and budget for next quarter.
- **Quarterly Business Review**: present outcomes vs. OKRs; agree next quarter plan.

**Exit criteria for Day 90:** clear, evidence-based answer to which 2–3 channels to double down on next quarter; North Star metric trending up; CAC/payback within target on scaled channels; compounding assets in place; operating cadence and approval workflow running smoothly; quarterly plan and budget approved for next 90 days.

## 12. Risks, Limitations, and Human Approval Points

### 12.1 Strategic Risks
- **Premature scaling**: spending big before PMF or before tracking is trustworthy. Mitigation: stage-gated budget unlocks, incrementality tests.
- **Channel monoculture**: over-reliance on one channel/platform. Mitigation: maintain a portfolio with experimental tier.
- **Brand drift**: AI-generated volume erodes voice. Mitigation: brand guardrail agent + human review on Tier 1+ assets.
- **Vanity metrics**: optimizing for impressions/likes vs. revenue. Mitigation: KPI tree enforces ladder to North Star.

### 12.2 Execution Risks
- **Content fatigue & creative burnout**: declining CTR/CVR. Mitigation: scheduled creative refresh, hypothesis-led testing.
- **Deliverability damage**: aggressive sending hurts inbox placement. Mitigation: list hygiene, suppression rules, warm-up SOP.
- **Compliance violations**: GDPR/CCPA/HIPAA/financial promotions. Mitigation: compliance guardrail, regional content rules, DPA-aware integrations.
- **Attribution illusions**: platform-reported numbers overstate impact. Mitigation: incrementality tests, blended view.

### 12.3 AI-Specific Risks
- **Hallucinations** in claims, stats, customer quotes. Mitigation: claim-source binding; if no source, agent must flag.
- **Prompt injection / data exfiltration** via scraped content. Mitigation: sandboxed scraping, content sanitization, allow-listed sources.
- **PII leakage**: never put raw PII into prompts; use tokenized references.
- **Bias and tone misfires**: cultural/regional sensitivity reviews before publishing in new markets.
- **Over-automation**: shipping at speed without insight. Mitigation: WIP limits, weekly retros, kill switches.

### 12.4 Operational Risks
- **Tooling fragmentation** causing data gaps. Mitigation: warehouse-first architecture, single source of truth.
- **Approval bottlenecks** slowing execution. Mitigation: tiered approvals; default-approve templates within guardrails.
- **Vendor/agency lock-in**. Mitigation: own the data, briefs, and creative library; standardize handoffs.

### 12.5 Human Approval Points (mandatory)
- Brand positioning and messaging changes.
- Launching a new channel or new market.
- Budget changes >20% or > a defined absolute threshold.
- Pricing, promotions, and discounting communications.
- Public statements, PR pitches, executive content.
- Partnerships, contracts, influencer agreements.
- Anything legal, regulated, or making competitive claims.
- Crisis communications and any negative-event response.
- Use of customer data for new use cases or new vendors.

### 12.6 Limitations to Acknowledge Upfront
- The agent's quality is bounded by the quality of the BCP and data access.
- It will not replace deep human judgment on brand soul, sensitive PR, and high-stakes strategic bets.
- Predictions are probabilistic; always validate with experiments before scaling.
- Some channels (events, sales-led enterprise motions) require human-led execution; the agent supports, not replaces.

## How to use this design

1. **Stand up the BCP and integrations** (Week 1).
2. **Spin up sub-agents incrementally** in this order: Discovery → Analytics → Strategy → Planner → Content/SEO → Paid → Lifecycle → Social → Experimentation → Partnerships/PR → CRO → Brand Guardrail → Orchestrator.
3. **Lock the cadence** (daily pulse, weekly scorecard, monthly review, quarterly strategy refresh).
4. **Enforce the goal cascade** so every shipped asset traces to a KPI and hypothesis.
5. **Compound**: turn every winning experiment into an SOP and every SOP into an automation.
